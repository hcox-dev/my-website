VTubers.TV is an open-source streaming, upload, and social platform designed for VTubers. It provides tools for broadcasting, avatar management, and fan interaction. The project emphasizes transparency, fairness, and user privacy, operating under the MIT License.

Overview

The platform enables VTubers to stream, upload, and collaborate within a creator-first ecosystem. It offers modern community tools, transparent monetization, and privacy-centric infrastructure.

What are VTubers?

Example of a virtual entertainer (VTuber) model in use.

VTubers (Virtual YouTubers) are online performers using animated avatars driven by motion capture or facial tracking. They stream gaming, music, and interactive content. Originating in Japan, the format has expanded globally through anime and gaming communities.

Goals

  • Provide community engagement tools such as polls, predictions, and raid systems.
  • Maintain open-source transparency and fair revenue sharing.
  • Enforce privacy-by-design and user data control.
  • Support VTuber-specific technologies and plugin ecosystems.
  • Encourage inclusivity through multilingual support and regional discovery.

Technology

Various VTuber characters used as examples in global media.
  • Next.js – frontend framework for React.
  • Supabase – SQL database and authentication.
  • Node.js – backend runtime for APIs.
  • FFmpeg – video processing for streaming.
  • Redis – caching and messaging.
  • Docker – deployment and scaling.

Features

  • Low-latency live streaming with chat and emotes.
  • Video uploads and adaptive playback.
  • Creator dashboards with analytics.
  • Low-fee revenue system and payout transparency.
  • Customizable creator profiles and avatars.
  • Social hub with posts and collaborations.

Industry context

Existing streaming platforms often impose high fees, restrictive algorithms, and invasive data policies. VTubers.TV aims to address these structural issues by decentralizing control and empowering creators.

Monetization

  • Membership subscriptions and donations.
  • Optional ad-sharing with transparent analytics.
  • Community-funded grant program (“Founders’ Pockets”).
  • Enterprise API access for agencies.

Philosophy

VTubers.TV positions itself as a community-owned alternative to corporate streaming platforms. It advocates for openness, fairness, and privacy — built by VTubers, for VTubers.
